Museum of Moving Parts

There are several ways to go about solving a problem or executing a task. Each part of the whole plays a pivotal role in the total success. Rube Goldberg, a famous comics artist created the concept of a machine in which it is intentionally designed to perform a simple task in an indirect and overcomplicated fashion. The Museum of Moving Parts gives homage to these machines and their attention to a single components role in completing a larger goal.

The Museum’s branding centrals around the white ‘M’ created out of found parts. Each exhibitor received a part along with the corresponding colors. There are 5 colors to the MMP brand-red, green, yellow, blue, and pink. The colors are bright yet pastel and reminisce of a child’s playroom. The colors can be seen throughout the museum, for example, on the website, the tickets, and the items sold in the store. The typeface is Cinetype by GT chosen for the sharp corners and mechanical-gear-like qualities.

The promotional video utilizes the brand’s colors and plays off of the childish tones of the museum. The instructions show the user how to create a simple moving parts machine and encourages them to buy a ticket to the museum to learn more about how to create these contraptions.

Thinker

Brand and concept creation for “Thinker,” a puzzle and toy design company that encourages adults to flex their brains by mailing challenging puzzles every month.

It is targeted to “the problem solvers, the self care takers, and the explorative game players,” otherwise known as thirty five to fifty year olds who subscribe to magazine/news outlets and or place a large priority in personal health.

The branding centers around the idea that keeping up mental agility isn’t something that has to be a task, but something playful and age appropriate. The cooler tones alongside the bright pop of yellow brings a sophistication to the color palette.

While a mature sense is pertinent in the visuals of the brand, the personal voice takes on a relaxed and informal tone to play the role of a friend and remind you that keeping your brain healthy can be fun!

IBM Plex Type Specimen

This is a type specimen book for the typeface IBM Plex. The book was to be in the form of a classic computer manual. The final book has 25 pages filled with differing layouts and imagery. Each page features a different aspect of the typeface.

I really wanted to draw attention to the shapes of the letters. The geometric shapes of the monospace make the typeface almost pictographic. I believed that the book didn't need any illustrations for the letters were beautiful as imagery in themselves. ​​​​​​​

There wasn't a need for chapter dividers; however, there were clear distinctions of sections in the book. I separated it by glyphs, forms, and numerals. While I didn't want to put formal chapter dividers I instead decided to use spreads that related to the oncoming content. For example a calculator signals the beginning of the Numerals section of the book.

Printed on the risograph with cornflower blue and pink. ​​

Please note that the scans are slightly yellow! While the photos are edited I did not want to take away from the natural color of the Risograph ink.


A full online scan of the book is available. Please email me if you would like to see it!

Recipe Book

A recipe book comprised of written essays and recipes of each student in the class. Bringing all recipes together into a class cook book, designed however chosen. Each student designed their own books with the collected content.

There is an emphasis on the class aspect of the book. Since the book is a collection of personal recipes of the class, it would make the most sense if it pays homage to that. There are tiny details including the professor's name on the back of the book, the class code, and the class directory included in the table of contents.

The book is 5.5"x8.5". The chapter dividers are printed on colored paper. The recipes in the book go down in roygbiv order. The color chosen correlates to an ingredient in the recipe.

There is a two column grid that is flexible into one. The recipes were set in a slightly larger font from the accompanying story for easier access to viewing while cooking. This also pertains to the recipe ingredients. They can easily be referenced as they are placed right next to the instructions in a large legible type size with measurements in the given section's color.
