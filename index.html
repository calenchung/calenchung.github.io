Museum of Moving Parts

There are several ways to go about solving a problem or executing a task. Each part of the whole plays a pivotal role in the total success. Rube Goldberg, a famous comics artist created the concept of a machine in which it is intentionally designed to perform a simple task in an indirect and overcomplicated fashion. The Museum of Moving Parts gives homage to these machines and their attention to a single components role in completing a larger goal.

The Museum’s branding centrals around the the white ‘M’ created out of found parts. Each exhibitor received a part along with the corresponding colors. There are 5 colors to the MMP brand-red, green, yellow, blue, and pink. The colors are bright yet pastel and reminisce of a child’s playroom. The colors can be seen throughout the museum, for example, on the website, the tickets, and the items sold in the store. The typeface is Cinetype by GT chosen for the sharp corners and mechanical-gear-like qualities.



The promotional video utilizes the brand’s colors and plays off of the childish tones of the museum. The instructions show the user how to create a simple moving parts machine and encourages them to buy a ticket to the museum to learn more about how to create these contraptions.

IBM Plex Type Specimen

This is a type specimen book for the typeface IBM Plex. The book was to be in the form of a classic computer manual. The final book has 25 pages filled with differing layouts and imagery. Each page features a different aspect of the typeface.

I really wanted to draw attention to the shapes of the letters. The geometric shapes of the monospace make the typeface almost pictographic. I believed that the book didn't need any illustrations for the letters were beautiful as imagery in themselves. ​​​​​​​

There wasn't a need for chapter dividers; however, there were clear distinctions of sections in the book. I separated it by glyphs, forms, and numerals. While I didn't want to put formal chapter dividers I instead decided to use spreads that related to the oncoming content. For example a calculator signals the beginning of the Numerals section of the book.

Printed on the risograph with cornflower blue and pink. ​​

Please note that the scans are slightly yellow! While the photos are edited I did not want to take away from the natural color of the Risograph ink

Recipe Book

A recipe book comprised of written essays and recipes of each student in the class. The task was to bring all recipes together into a class cook book, designed however chosen. Each student designed their own books with the collected content.

I decided to go in the direction of having a large emphasis on the class aspect of the book. Since the book is a collection of personal recipes to the class I found it would make the most sense if it payed homaged to that. So I included tiny things like including my professor's name on the back of the book, the class code, and the class directory included in the table of contents.

The book is 5.5"x8.5". The chapter dividers are printed on colored paper. The recipes in the book go down in roygbiv order. The color correlates to an ingredient in the recipe.

After a lot of trial and error I decided to go with a two column grid that was flexible into one. The recipes were set in a slightly larger font from the recipe story so that it is easier to view while cooking. This also pertains to the recipe ingredients. They can easily be referenced as they are placed right next to the instructions in a large legible type size with measurements in the given section's color.

Synergy Rebranding

The Synergy Company is a nutrition and supplements company based in Utah. This fake rebranding takes The Synergy Company and turns it into Synergy. It focuses specifically on their veggie powders line. The rebranding is very lightly based off of The Synergy Company's original products and logo.

Initially I was met with quite a confusion on how to revamp this brand that really had no clear direction. My first decision was to focus solely on a certain line that they were selling. I did some research on the company and luckily enough they had an about page that told me all of their goals. It also happened to have a logo page describing the reasoning for their decision on the illustration of the old algae fossil. The research was brief and was mainly used to direct myself with questions that arose while learning about the company.

The new Synergy logo refers to the company's mission of health and its original logo of an algae fossil. The company had put emphasis on the idea of the complexity of the original "conveying the essence of energy". While I didn't want to keep the logo as complicated as it is now, I still wanted to give the idea of interconnectedness. The spiral into the heart shows the journey into health and the energy that comes from within.

Comedy Fest Poster

The brief for this project was to create a 24x36 inch event poster calendar that listed multiple events and their corresponding dates/times. On the back of the calendar is a poster summarizing the event.

I chose to go with the New York Comedy Festival, an event that was about to happen at the time of the project. A challenge for me was the massive amount of events happening with dozens of locations and times. I knew there was no way that I could list them side by side and fit within the parameters. I decided that I would make a key to avoid double stating a location.

The rules were simple: only 2 typefaces allowed, 3 total type sizes, and a limit of 2 colors.

There is a large focus on the interaction of opening the poster. Initially the user is met with a 12x8.5 inch stating the name of the event which slowly opens up to unveil more information. I wanted to walk the user through on how to read the poster as they are opening it. (Corresponding gif)
